About

CodeJam is a 48-hour team-based programming competition fueled by great minds and energy drinks.

McGill University's Electrical, Computer and Software Engineering Student Society (ECSESS) is collaborating with some of Montreal's biggest software companies to make a challenging, real-world application problem for you to solve. ECSESS's largest event is back for its 7th year and this time it's all about Human Computer Interaction.

FAQs

What is CodeJam? When? Where?

tl;dr The best three days of your life.
McGill Code Jam is a 48-hours coding competition organized by the Electrical, Computer and Software Engineering Student Society (ECSESS). The competition will be held in Trottier building at McGill’s downtown campus and lasts from November 18 to 20. The opening ceremony will be at 6PM in TR0100 on Friday, during which the challenges will be presented.

Who can jam code?

Anyone can enter Code Jam! Organized by ECSESS, the competition is catered to the students in ECSE department, BUT Code Jam is open to all undergraduate students in other engineering departments and other faculties as well. For graduate students, your team must consist of at least one undergraduate student and for students who are not from McGill, your team must have at least one McGill student on it.

How do I enter?

You can sign up via the form available under “Join” tile. Make sure everyone on your team register before the form closes on Nov 18!

How big are the teams? Can I jam alone?

Teams should be between 2-4 people. No lone wolves, fam.

What are the differences between Junior & Senior Challenge? I’m a new coder.

We have heard your feedback and this year we are beginning a BRAND NEW junior challenge that will run in parallel with the regular (senior) challenge. If you have never participated in a coding competition, this option would be a great opportunity for you to experience problem solving through software! The junior challenge follows the same rules as the senior counterpart, but the problem will be less demanding to suit those with less programming experience. Of course, the prizes will be different for different challenges.

Will food be provided?

Absolutely! Food is the essence of every software. We will provide swags, caffeine and every meal from Friday’s dinner to Sunday’s lunch, as well as a dinner on Monday November 21st for the closing ceremonies.

Why do I have to submit my Resume/CV?

Code Jam is sponsored by some of Montreal’s top technology companies. As part of the sponsorship package, your resumes will be submitted to these companies for potential career opportunities. Some of the sponsoring companies will also be present at the Tech Fair before the opening ceremony.

What if I have another question?

You can message us your question using mylivechat widget on the bottom left corner of the desktop website. Of course, feel free to reach out to us via Facebook or email us at ecsess.vpexternal@mcgilleus.ca.
